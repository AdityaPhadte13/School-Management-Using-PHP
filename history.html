History





On 2nd Oct 1911 on the auspicious day of Vijayadashmi Goa Vidyaprasarak Mandal & its First Education Institution A.J.de Almeida High School then known as “Colegio Jose de Almeida” was founded by Pranacharya Dr. Ramchandra Pandurang Vaidya alias Dr Dada Vaidya, Shri Sitaram Vishwambar Kerkar & Shri Vinayak Sarjyotishi. The School was housed in the edifice owned by Dr. Dada Vaidya where the present building of GVM’s KG & Primary School is located. The Present building of A. J. de Almeida was completed & inaugurated on the occasion of Golden Jubilee Celebration on Oct 1961 at the hands of Governor General H. E. Vassalo e Silva. Goa Vidyaprasarak Mandal (GVM) was originally named as Liga da Propaganda de Insrucao em Goa and was founded on the auspicious day of "Vijayadashmi" in the year 1911 A.D. (which was Monday, 2nd of October), by some great visionaries of those times, namely, Dr. Dada Vaidya, Shri Sitaram Kerkar and Shri Vinayak Sarjyotishi. Goa was passing through a difficult historical period, as the entire region along with Daman & Diu was under the Portuguese rule. Fortunately, the establishment of Republic in Portugal in the year 1910 created a window of opportunity for the Goan community to establish their own institutes. Driven with an idea of creating a self sustainable and economically independent society, the Founding Fathers of GVM started a School in 1911 and named it after a Portuguese Parliamentarian Dr. Anton Jose de Almeida who had defended the rights of local Goans in Portuguese parliament.
